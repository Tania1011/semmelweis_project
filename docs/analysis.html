Conclusions

  • Clinic 1 (medical students performing autopsies) had consistently higher death proportions than Clinic 2 (midwife students) from 1841–1846.
  • After Semmelweis mandated handwashing in June 1847, deaths fell by roughly -0.084 in absolute proportion.
  • Bootstrap 95 % CI: [-0.1017, -0.067] — equivalent to a 10.17–6.7 percentage-point reduction.
  • Welch's t-test: t = 9.6101, p = 1.45e-15. Result is statistically significant.
